# Grill Me: Bakery Second Location

## Motivation

What's driving the timing on this — is the second location something you've been planning for, or did a specific opportunity (a lease, a competitor leaving, a neighborhood change) pull you toward it now?

The first location has been profitable for 3 years — is the profitability stable and growing, or has it plateaued, and how does that trajectory factor into your decision to expand now rather than later?

## Design Decisions

You're replicating the same menu — what's your read on whether the new neighborhood's customer base matches the profile of your current location's regulars?

The new location is 20 minutes away — how are you thinking about the supply and logistics chain between the two? Will production stay centralized at location one, or will location two operate independently?

What makes this specific space the right fit — is the lease terms, the square footage, the neighborhood foot traffic pattern, or something else driving the selection?

## People and Process

You're planning to hire a manager for the new location — what does that manager's authority look like day-to-day? What decisions will they make independently versus escalating to you?

How involved are you planning to be at location two during the first 6 months, and what does that mean for how much of your attention stays on location one?

Do you have a candidate in mind for the manager role, or are you hiring externally — and what's your timeline for having that person in place relative to the opening date?

## Operational Readiness

The first location has been running for 3 years — what operational knowledge exists only in your head or in informal practice that hasn't been documented, and how are you planning to transfer that to the new manager?

What's your training plan for the new location's staff, and how long before opening are you planning to have them onboarded?

## Financing

You mentioned a small business loan to cover the buildout — what does your financial model look like for how long the new location needs to operate before it services that debt on its own?

What's your break-even timeline assumption, and how sensitive is that number to variables like slower-than-expected foot traffic conversion or higher-than-projected staffing costs?

If location two underperforms in the first year, what's the impact on location one's finances — are the two financially isolated or does one backstop the other?

## Risk and Exit

If the new manager isn't working out 4 months in, what's your plan — do you step in directly, or do you have another person ready to step up?

What happens to the loan obligation if the second location doesn't reach profitability within your projected window?

What would have to be true for you to decide the second location isn't viable and close it — and have you thought through what that process looks like?
